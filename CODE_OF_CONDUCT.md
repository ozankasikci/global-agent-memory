# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in Global Agent Memory a harassment-free experience
for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity,
sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education,
socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion,
or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse,
inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include demonstrating
empathy, respecting differing opinions and experiences, giving and accepting
constructive feedback, accepting responsibility, and focusing on what is best for the
community.

Unacceptable behavior includes sexualized language or imagery, trolling, insulting or
derogatory comments, public or private harassment, publishing others' private
information without permission, and other conduct that could reasonably be considered
inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Enforcement responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards. They
may remove, edit, or reject contributions, comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues,
and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces and when an individual is
officially representing the community in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported
privately through a
[GitHub security advisory](https://github.com/ozankasikci/global-agent-memory/security/advisories/new).
All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. The privacy and
security of reporters will be respected.

## Enforcement guidelines

Maintainers will follow the four-level Community Impact Guidelines: correction,
warning, temporary ban, and permanent ban. The consequence will reflect the impact and
persistence of the behavior.
